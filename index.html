Stream Town

The Specs:

    Engine: Unity
    Language: C#
    Role: Project Lead / Lead Programmer / Designer

Overview:

Stream Town is an online twitch-based RTS Idle Game. It is a game that is played solely on the Twitch streaming platform. It is a rather idle game where players will create a character, select a role and watch your player go! The objective of the game is to build up a sprawling town and protect it from night time attacks. There are many roles to choose from including resource collection roles like logger, miner, gatherer as well as defensive roles such as defender, ranger and soldier.

Players can level up their characters over time and spend skillpoints leveling up whichever role they see fit. leveling up roles will provide their character with global stat boosts that will improve their overall performance.



Project Development:

This project was started by myself and my brother in 2020 and we worked on it for a few months during our limited free time. My brother took control of all of the 3D models and animations while I covered the rest of the project. Together we have created something we are both happy with.


My Role:

Twitch Integration: Connected chat commands to the game.
Chat Commands: Created an array of chat commands for players to interact with.
Channel Points: Allowed users to spend channel points on fun rewards.
Artificial Intelligence: Developed State Machine based AI to control player characters.
Procedural Generation: Generating the world,resources, enemies and lakes.
Building: Developed placement and construction of buildings.
Text Parsing: Extensive text parsing to allow for flexible command types.

How to Play:

The game is available 24/7 over at Twitch.

Escaping Project Paragon

The Specs:

    Engine: Unity
    Language: C#
    Role: Lead Programmer

Overview:

Escaping Project Paragon (EPP) was an end of year assignemnt at AIE Melbourne for our first year of the course. EPP is a stealth based game that requires the player to sneak around guards and escape a cloning and human testing facility. The player has limited movement controls with a walk and sprint. The player also has the option of distracting guards by throwing glass beakers.

Throughout the game, the player will collect keycards that are required to progress though each zone, being careful to avoid detection from guards and cameras around the facility. The player can collect notes around the facility to learn more about where and who they are as well as discover lore built into the world's design.





Project Development:

This project involved a team of 9 people; 3 Designers, 1 Programmer, 5 Artists. Pre-production began on the 8th of September and the final project was finished on 1st of December. Our team managed to stay on track throughout all milestones; alpha, beta and gold. Keeping to our schedule allowed us to make sure we had a project that we were happy to call finished and show to other people.


My Role:

Artificial Intelligence: Developed State Machine based AI to control Guards.
Sensors: Visual and Audio sensors for the AI to detect the player.
Messaging System: Communication between Cameras and Guards, integrated with the state machine.
Waypoint System: System for allowing path's to be easily created for Guards.
Saving and Loading: Binary File based saving and loading of game data.
Audio Setup: Integrated Audio and set up 3D sound and volume mixers.
Build Production: Handled the building and release of the game files. Tools: Developed tools to assist Designers, waypoint tools, checkpoint tools.







How to Play:

The game is available to download here.



Snowed In

The Specs:

    Engine: Unity
    Language: C#
    Role: Lead Programmer

Overview:

Snowed In: The Campfire is a survival adventure game about a father protecting his son in a vicious snow storm. The player must navigate through an expansive wilderness littered with derelict buildings and abandoned roads. It is dark and the father must carry his son in search of warmth and light. The player can keep himself and the child safe by utilizing firepits placed around the world. The fires are kept lit by collecting wood and piling them onto the firepits.

The player must also keep their torch lit throughout the game, taking wood from the firepits, allowing the player to pierce through the darkness and wade off wolves that will try to attack him. If the player's light runs out, their chances of survival reach near zero.



Project Development:

This project developed over a single weekend for the GDL July 2021 Game Jam by first year students at AIE Melbourne. The team consisted of 4 designers, 2 programmers and 3 artists.


My Role:

Lead Programmer: Delegated tasks and organized work flow of the programming.
Artificial Intelligence: Developed State Machine based AI to dictate wolf behaviour.
Fire System: Collection of wood, burning wood on fires, lighting torch.
Torch: Used detection to deter wolves and make them flee from the player.
Checkpoints: Firepits acted as checkpoints for the player.
Carry: Carrying of the baby to and from safe zones.
Post Process Integration: Adjusted Post Processing based on state of the game.
Tools: Developed AI and Checkpoint tools to assist designers.


How to Play:

The game is available to download here.

2D Adventures

The Specs:

    Engine: Unity
    Language: C#
    Role: Lead Programmer

Overview:

This project was developed for a first year school assignment where we were tasked to develop a game based only in User Interface using the Unity Game Engine. Using this limitation, I decided I wanted to develop something that would challenge me while I got to learn about tool development. The game itself is somewhat simple in nature. You create a save file, name your character and choose your class. Upon entering the game, you walk around using UI buttons and randomly encounter enemies that you can choose to fight. You level up your character and progress through different maps fighting different encounters, collecting loot along the way.



Project Development:

This project developed over three weeks half way through 2021. We were tasked to develop a UI game in Unity as a solo project. Over the course of the three weeks, I developed an exapansive tool to aid in the development of items, creatures, maps and more in the game by simply pressing buttons. This sped up the development of the world as it saved time not creating invidivual objects and looking through the unity inspector for each individual variable.




My Role:

Combat: Developed a combat system for fighting encounters.
Inventory: Storing, equipping and inspecting items gained from encounters.
Saving/Loading: Saving and Loading of multiple character save files.
Tools: Developed an extensive tool for easily creating items.
Database: Set up a file based database for all entities in the game.
Map System: Easy to edit map system for switching maps and encounters.

Tool Development:

One of my new found passions with programming is tool development. I find being able to make development easier for myself and others is satisfying and enjoyable. They often require a different mindset to gameplay programming and I find it gives me different challenges to work through.

Renamer Tool

Quite often when I'm looking for ideas for developing tools I ask other programmers and designers what sort of tools would they like to see. A design teacher at AIE was discussing how a renaming tool would be very helpful in Unity.

I took this idea up and decided to create an extensive renaming tool as shown in the picture below.

Some of the features this renamer tool offers includes the general prefix and sufix, but also offers a range of 'find and replace', insert at, remove at options as well.

It even includes options for numbering items with the extra option of aligning with Unity's naming standards. The tool also gives a preview of the changes before you apply them.

Dungeon Generation

One thing that really fascinated me with games was procedural generation, especially dungeon generation. As a kid I was always excited about learning how it was done.

My first attempt at dungeon generation was in Game Maker like the project above. I had a 2D world where a "walker" would walk through the world, generating hallways and rooms as it went. It was simple in nature but to a younger me, it was incredible that this was something I could do!



Once I grasped the 3D environments possible in Unity, I decided to try out dungeon generation again, this time of course, in 3D! With this in mind I got my brother to develop a few modular pieces, a couple of characters and away I went. This dungeon generator would generate pieces as the characters went along in a top down view. Culling away the previously generated rooms behind it that were no longer visible.
This project also taught me a lot about Unity's Nav Mesh system, using it generate nav meshes at runtime, and getting agents to move along the endlessly generating dungeon. It also allowed me to develop my AI skills further, having the agents running along and fighting enemies as they go.
